Energy Harvesting Management Circuit

A project for ECE 547 "VLSI Design" - Fall Semester 2011
Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering
University of Maine

Designed by: Praveen Gunturi

The energy management system (EMS) plays an important role when using an energy harvester to power an another circuit. The EMS converts an input current into a output voltage that can be regulated to provide power to another circuit. An input current to the EMS is provided by an Energy harvesting circuit. This current can vary from few nanoamperes to a few microamperes. The EMS circuit stores the charge on a large capacitor. When a first charge threshold point is reached, the capacitor dumps its charge to power the output circuit. This continues until a second charge threshold point is reached at which time the capacitor stops discharging and then starts charging again. This process is then repeated. The EMS circuit is designed to provide 2mA of current for up to four seconds at a voltage greater than 1.8V.

The project report contains a description of the project and details of the design and layout.
